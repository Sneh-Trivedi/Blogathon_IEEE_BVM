Blogathon

Bloggers, writers and tech-enthusiasts, it gives us immense pleasure to announce that IEEE BVM SB is having a Blog writing competition, "Blogathon". It is quoted that "A professional writer is an amateur who didn't quit", and we here at BVM SB, encourage you to do the same. In this time of countrywide lockdown, implementing and showcasing knowledge is just as important as gaining knowledge. This is not purely a technical blogathon, we urge you to submit your entries in the non-technical part of the event as well and give your best shot. The experts that are set to evaluate the entries are no stranger to this and are excelling in their domains of research. And lastly, to support the efforts of our Prime Minister in the battle against COVID-19, we will be donating the funds raised to the PM Cares Fund.

Rules & Guidelines

TimeLine

  • 26th April 2020 (12 am IST): Registration Starts
  • 30th April 2020 (12 am IST): Submission Portal Opens
  • 2nd May 2020 (11:30 pm IST): Last Day for Registrations
  • 3rd May 2020 (11:30 pm IST): Last Day for Submission
  • 4th May 2020: Review Starts
  • 6th May 2020: Winner Declaration

Registration

  • Registration will be done through the following ways,
    • Website: blogathon.com
    • Townscript: Register
  • Registration Starts from 26th April 2020 (12:00 am IST).
  • Registration will be open till 2nd May 2020 (11:59 pm IST).

Submission

  • For submission, you'll have to provide us with the website URL of your Blog in the Submission Portal
  • The Submission of blogs will be in two Categories:
    • Technical Blogs
    • Non-Technical Blogs
  • You may choose any topic as per your choice.
  • With the confirmation mail, you will get a link for the submission portal
  • Submission will be open from 30th April 2020 (12:00 am IST) and will be closed on 3rd May 2020 (11:59 pm IST).
  • Only one submission will be accepted per participant.
  • Every participant will have to create a new website on WORDPRESS and that website should contain only one blog.
    • The webpage should be created after the 26th of April 2020 and also the date should be visible at the end of the blog. (Which is available in the website setting in WordPress)
  • The blog shall be written in the English Language.

Review

  • The review will be done in three phases.
  • Phase 1: Primary Filtration
    • Based on Plagiarism
    • The content of the blog should have at least 75% Uniqueness.
    • All the blogs with uniqueness less than 75% will be disqualified
  • Phase 2: In phase 2 content-based review will be done, Marks will be given considering the following parameters.
    • Content Quality
      • It includes Clarity, Engagement, Delivery, Grammar and Readability, Audience approachability.
    • Topic relevance
      • Consistency and depth of the Topic, Literature review, proofreading, factual information.
    • Design
      • Includes Aesthetics of the webpage, Ease of Navigation, SEO (Search Engine Optimization), Use of Images, GIF, hyperlinks, Sub sectional information.
    • Considering all the above factors marks will be provided by the review committee.
    • For blogs with different topics, field expert will be reviewing and will be giving the marks.
  • Phase 3: If there is any tie for blogs with two different or relevant topics then the phase 3 review will be done.
    • In this phase, we'll send the blogs for the expert review and the result will be declared.
  • Review of Blogs will be done by IEEE Magazines and Newsletter Article Professionals.

Result

  • The result will be declared through mails.
  • Top three blogs from each category will get,
    • An assured prize.
    • Feature On the IEEE BVM Website.
    • Feature in the IEEE BVM News Letter.
    • All blogs will be rewarded by an acknowledgement certificate

BVM Engineering College

Birla Vishvakarma Mahavidyalaya Engineering College, an Autonomous Institute managed by Charutar Vidya Mandal was established in 1948 from donations made by the Birla Education Trust on the behest of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, the first Home Minister of independent India. The college was inaugurated by Lord Mountbatten, the Governor General of India on 14 June 1948, and rose to prominence under the stewardship of Prof. Junnarkar and Prof. K.M. Dholakia. It was one of the few colleges in India that adopted the progressive credit system of relative grading in India. The college has awarded degrees to over 20,000 graduates.

IEEE BVM SB

IEEE BVM SB was established on 29th August 2016. The main moto of Student Branch is to connects engineers from various department of the institute to come together, identify problem statements and act accordingly to improvise and promote Technical Activities which can in turn be formed for "Advancing Technology for Humanity". It aims to give students the cutting-edge skills, confidence and intellect with which they can easily face the competitive world and contribute for human welfare by organizing competitions, workshops, expert talks, seminars and symposiums on different technical topics.
